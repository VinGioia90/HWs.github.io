Markov-switching (MS) models represent a flexible modelling framework in which an unobserved state variable determines the membership of observations to distinct regimes. Conditional on the regime, the data-generating mechanism can be described using relatively simple stochastic models. For comprehensive treatments of Markov-switching models, see FrĂĽhwirth-Schnatter (2006) and Zucchini et al. (2017). The MS model is specified by two processes:

  • Latent stochastic process: $\{S_t\}_{t \geq 0}$, with $S_t\in\{1,2, \ldots, K\}$, with $K$ the number of states/regimes
  • Observable stochastic process: $\{Y_t\}_{t \geq 1}$, whose realizations are $y_t$, for $t=1, \ldots, T$


      • Latent stochastic process


      We assume a latent process ruled by a first-order Markov chain structure with homogeneous transition probabilities. In formulas, the process is specified through $\delta_{jk} = {\rm Pr}\left(S_t=k|S_{t-1}=j\right)$, for $j,k = 1, \ldots, K$, that are the entries of the stochastic $K \times K$ transition probability matrix $\boldsymbol \Delta$. Finally, the process $\{S_t\}_{t \geq 0}$ is fully specified through the initial probability vector, $\boldsymbol \pi_0$, with $\pi_{0k} = {\rm Pr}(S_0=k)$, for $k = 1, \ldots, K$.

      In this work, we only consider two different sparse transition probability matrix structures $\boldsymbol \Delta^{(l)}$, with $l\in \{1,2\}$ denoting the maximum step-length transition. More precisely, we consider:

      TPM1 model

      Only allowing transitions among adjacent regimes, that is $\delta^{(1)}_{jk}\neq 0$ if and only if $j=1,\ldots,K, \qquad k\in\omega^{(1)}_j$, with $\omega^{(1)}_j= \{k:\max(1,j-1)\le k\le \min(j+1,K)\}$. This leads to

      $$ \boldsymbol{\Delta}^{(1)}= \begin{pmatrix} \delta^{(1)}_{11} & \delta^{(1)}_{12} & 0 & \cdots & 0\\ \delta^{(1)}_{21} & \delta^{(1)}_{22} & \delta^{(1)}_{23} & \ddots & 0\\ 0 & \ddots & \ddots & \ddots & \ddots\\ \vdots & \ddots & \delta^{(1)}_{K-1K-2} & \delta^{(1)}_{K-1K-1} & \delta^{(1)}_{K-1K}\\ 0 & \cdots & 0 & \delta^{(1)}_{KK-1} & \delta^{(1)}_{KK} \end{pmatrix} $$

      TPM2 model

      Allowing transitions among regimes with a maximum distance of two steps, that is $\delta^{(2)}_{jk}\neq 0$ if and only if $j=1,\ldots,K, \qquad k\in\omega^{(2)}_j$, with $\omega^{(2)}_j= \{k:\max(1,j-2)\le k\le \min(j+2,K)\}$. This leads to

      $$ \boldsymbol{\Delta}^{(2)}= \begin{pmatrix} \delta^{(2)}_{11} & \delta^{(2)}_{12} & \delta^{(2)}_{13} & 0 & \cdots & 0\\ \delta^{(2)}_{21} & \delta^{(2)}_{22} & \delta^{(2)}_{23} & \delta^{(2)}_{24} & \ddots & 0\\ 0 & \ddots & \ddots & \ddots & \ddots & \cdots\\ \vdots & \ddots & \delta^{(2)}_{K-1K-3} & \delta^{(2)}_{K-1K-2} & \delta^{(2)}_{K-1K-1} & \delta^{(2)}_{K-1K}\\ 0 & \cdots & 0 & \delta^{(2)}_{KK-2} & \delta^{(2)}_{KK-1} & \delta^{(2)}_{KK} \end{pmatrix} $$


      During the estimation process, the transition probabilities are modelled via (multi-)logit link function, that is $\nu^{(l)}_{jk} = {\rm logit}\left(\delta^{(l)}_{jk} \right)$, for $j = 1,\ldots, K$, and $k \in \tilde \omega^{(l)}_j$, with $\tilde \omega^{(l)}_j = \omega^{(l)}_j \setminus \{j\}$, and $l\in \{1,2\}$. \\ Observable stochastic process
