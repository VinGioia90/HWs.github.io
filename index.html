Workflow and website description

This research project was born about three years ago, when three academics, passionate about splines, climate, and statistics, combined their research interests. That is the beauty of research.

During the literature review, we discovered that the first paper to introduce the term Heat Wave was titled "Heat-Waves in Asia: Sunshine at Trieste" (Gold, 1912). Quite the coincidence, given our affiliation. That paper offered a simplistic definition of heat wave, which has since been refined to account for both the anomaly of temperatures and their temporal extent.

In this work, we compare the proposed model-based heat wave classification approach with the most widely used definition, based on the empirical threshold-based classification approach of Perkins and Alexander (2013).

This website accompanies the posters presented at SIS-FENStatS 2026 (Rome) and ISBA 2026 (Nagoya), providing further details on the:
